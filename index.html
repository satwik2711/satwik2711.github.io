About

I'm Satwik — I build AI systems that process visa documents at scale and then spend my research time figuring out why those same systems shouldn't be trusted blindly.

As an AI Research Engineer at VFS Global, I work on production document intelligence — 180K+ documents daily, multi-country visa processing, the kind of pipeline where a wrong extraction doesn't just fail a test case, it delays someone's life. That context shapes how I think about AI: models are impressive right up until you need them to know when they're wrong, explain why they failed, or recover gracefully when the input is messy and the stakes are real.

My research lives at the intersection of reliability, reasoning, and interpretability in large models. I'm interested in how language and multimodal systems behave once they're deployed outside clean benchmark settings: how they reason, how they fail, how they express uncertainty, and how we can build verification, correction, and evaluation mechanisms around them. A lot of my recent work has focused on uncertainty quantification, but I care more broadly about the interpretability and reliability of large models — making them safe to deploy in contexts where "usually right" isn't good enough.

I'm targeting Fall 2027 PhD programs to go deeper on these questions.

S. Pandey, et al.

SELFDOUBT: Uncertainty Quantification for Reasoning LLMs via the Hedge-to-Verify Ratio

Proposed an O(1) black box uncertainty framework that extracts behavioral hedge/verify signals from reasoning traces, significantly outperforming Semantic Entropy on discrimination (p=0.001) at 10x lower cost; a zero-hedge gate achieves 96.1% precision across 7 models and 3 benchmarks.

2026 Submitted to UAI 2026

S. Raghu, S. Pandey

Don't Blink: Evidence Collapse during Multimodal Reasoning

Identified a universal evidence collapse phenomenon in reasoning VLMs, observing visual attention drops up to 90.8% during generation and discovered a task-conditional failure regime where confident but visually disengaged predictions are hazardous on sustained visual reference tasks but benign on symbolic tasks.

Repair of Thought: Advancing Automated Program Repair through a Dual-Model Reasoning Framework

Introduced a function-level APR framework achieving an SOTA 83.1% plausible repair rate on Defects4J, with an automated verification pipeline combining AST alignment, control-flow symbolic analysis, and semantic checks.
